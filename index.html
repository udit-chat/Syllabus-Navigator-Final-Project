01 — The Problem

Silent failures in
academic administration

It is 11 PM. A student is hunting for one stable fact in a 15-page PDF. The syllabus says one thing. The calendar says another. Neither is wrong — but both cannot be right.

The human cost

A student loses marks because a due date says "Thursday, October 12th" but October 12th is a Wednesday. A TA applies the wrong grading scale because the syllabus contradicts university policy. These errors are invisible until they cause real damage.

Why base AI fails here

A base language model is a yes-man. Ask it to summarize a flawed syllabus and it faithfully reproduces the errors. It lacks the computational skepticism to look outside the document and verify facts against an authoritative source of truth.

The cost of non-existence: $4,500 retaken courses, hours of TA labor answering identical emails, and students trapped in expensive uncertainty — all caused by errors that were always there, just never caught.

02 — How It Works

Four steps from
PDF to verdict

RAG + Prompt Engineering — two core generative AI techniques — combined into an agentic compliance auditor.

01

Ingest & Chunk

PDFs read with pypdf, split into 500-character overlapping chunks with 50-char overlap using a custom Python splitter. Overlap prevents boundary information loss.

02

Embed & Index

Each chunk converted to a vector embedding using Google's text-embedding-004 model and stored in a FAISS similarity index. 39 chunks indexed per syllabus.

03

Retrieve & Audit

5 most relevant policy chunks retrieved via RAG similarity search. A structured prompt with temperature=0 instructs Gemini 2.5 Flash to act as a strict compliance auditor.

04

Flag & Report

Structured output — FLAG type, description, location, severity — parsed into a formatted Discrepancy Report. Performance evaluated with Retrieval Recall against human ground truth.

03 — Architecture

System design

Every component is visible, explainable, and reproducible. Temperature=0 ensures the same syllabus always produces the same flags.

04 — Key Findings

Real errors in
real documents

Every finding produced automatically. None were planted. All 6 courses had at least one compliance issue.

High · Policy Conflict
DAMG 6210 — Database Design

Grading scale contradicts university policy

Syllabus awards A at 94%+. University requires 95%+. A student scoring 94.5% gets a different grade depending on which document their professor follows. Direct GPA impact.

High · Date Issue
INFO 6105 — Data Science Methods

Entire schedule uses wrong semester dates

A Spring 2025 syllabus contains a schedule running September–December (Fall 2024 dates). All 15 weeks are misaligned. The Assignment Due column is completely blank.

High · Date Issue
INFO 6150 — Web Design

Class scheduled during Spring Break

Class 8 is listed on 03/03/2025 — the first day of Spring Break. University policy: no classes during the week of March 3, 2025.

High · Date Issue
CSYE 7280 — UX Design & Testing

Term year mismatch on page 1

Course information reads "Term and Year: Fall 2024" while the header says "SPRING 25" and the schedule shows Spring 2025 dates — a direct contradiction in the same document.

High · Date Issue
INFO 7374 — Operational Risk

Week 9 class scheduled on Spring Break

Week 9 is dated 3/4/2025, directly within Spring Break (week of March 3, 2025). University policy: no classes that week. Students cannot know this from the syllabus alone.

Medium · Policy Conflict
INFO 7375 — Prompt Engineering

Late policy contradicts university standard

Syllabus applies 5% per day deduction. University standard: no credit for late work submitted without prior faculty communication. These are fundamentally incompatible policies.
